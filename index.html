The Epic at a Glance

The Shiva Trilogy reimagines the revered deity as a mortal hero whose choices forge legends. Across three sweeping novels, Shiva is drawn from the icy Tibetan plains into the heart of Meluha, confronting warfare, political intrigue, and the moral consequences of power.

Author Amish Tripathi

The Books

The Immortals of Meluha

Published 2010

Shiva, a fierce warrior from Tibet, is drawn into the utopian empire of Meluha. When his throat turns blue after drinking Somras, he is hailed as the Neelkanth—savior of the Suryavanshi people. Torn between belief and duty, Shiva leads Meluha against the Chandravanshis and their mysterious Naga allies, learning that destiny is shaped by choice.

The Secret of the Nagas

Published 2011

Shiva's pursuit of a Naga assassin endangers those he loves most. His quest leads eastward to the land of Branga, where he must cure a deadly plague and confront revelations about Sati's hidden past. Allies and enemies swap places as the truth behind the Nagas emerges.

The Oath of the Vayuputras

Published 2013

The saga culminates with Shiva discovering the catastrophic consequences of Somras. Guided by the Vayuputras, he wages a righteous war against Meluha's leadership. Personal tragedy fuels his resolve, and the destruction of the old order paves the way for legend.

Enduring Themes

Destiny vs. Choice

Shiva's rise as the Neelkanth questions whether heroes are born or made. His decisions—and their consequences—shape the myth that surrounds him.

Balance of Good and Evil

The trilogy frames good and evil as situational, urging readers to examine morality through the lens of balance, context, and responsibility.

Legacy and Myth-Making

Each victory and sacrifice is immortalized by those who remain, revealing how legends are woven from lived experiences and collective memory.
