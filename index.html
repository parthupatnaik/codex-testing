The Epic at a Glance

The Shiva Trilogy reimagines the revered deity as a mortal hero whose choices forge legends. Across three sweeping novels, Shiva is drawn from the icy Tibetan plains into the heart of Meluha, confronting warfare, political intrigue, and the moral consequences of power.

Author Amish Tripathi
Genres Mythological fiction, fantasy adventure
Trilogy 2010 — 2013

Moments that Define the Legend

A swift journey through the trilogy’s pivotal turning points.

  1. Book I Shiva arrives in Meluha and drinks Somras, revealing the blue throat of prophecy.
  2. Book II An assassin’s strike unravels a conspiracy, guiding Shiva to the hidden Naga capital.
  3. Book III The source of evil is exposed, forcing a choice that reshapes the moral order of India.

Book Showcase

Three movements. One legend carried through time.

The Immortals of Meluha

2010 · Birth of the Neelkanth

Arriving as a refugee, Shiva is thrust into the utopian empire of Meluha. When Somras turns his throat blue, he becomes the fated savior tasked with confronting the Chandravanshis—only to discover morality is far from absolute.

The Secret of the Nagas

2011 · Shadows of the Serpent

Shiva journeys east to confront the mysterious Nagas, only to uncover buried truths about his allies and enemies. The war for balance expands beyond empires into the realm of the divine.

The Oath of the Vayuputras

2013 · The Final Reckoning

The concluding chapter leads Shiva to face the source of evil itself. Sacrifice, loyalty, and destiny collide as the fate of humanity balances on a single choice.

Key Allies & Adversaries

Nuanced companions who shape the Neelkanth's path.

Sati

Warrior princess of Meluha and Shiva's partner. Her resolve, compassion, and unflinching honour ground the trilogy's emotional core.

Parvateshwar

The general of the Meluhan army whose loyalty is earned, not given. His evolving allegiance mirrors the trilogy's exploration of duty and truth.

The Nagas

Outcast guardians with concealed identities. Their secrets illuminate the moral grey zones that force Shiva to question blind faith.

Themes that Resonate

Why this story endures across generations.

Dharma & Choice

The prophecy of the Neelkanth collides with Shiva's skepticism. Every decision he makes—rather than fate alone— defines the legend that survives him.

Science as Divinity

Somras, medicine, and engineered cities blur the lines between magic and technology, showing how knowledge can both heal and corrupt societies.

Balance of Good and Evil

The trilogy reframes good and evil as perspectives shaped by need, encouraging empathy, accountability, and the courage to change course.

Legacy & Myth-Making

Chronicles, songs, and survivors ensure Shiva's story endures—reminding us that legends are woven from imperfect people and impossible choices.
